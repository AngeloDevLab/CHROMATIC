# 7. Story & Narrative

## 7.1 Backstory

Eons ago, the world of a highly advanced civilization — somewhere beyond this world — was attacked by the Darkness: an ancient, mystical force capable of devouring entire worlds and draining them of color. As a last resort, the civilization created the Guardians: humanoid energy beings, bound to color and light as a counterforce to the Darkness. It could never be fully destroyed, only pushed back.

Since the Darkness does not threaten just one world but moves from world to world, the civilization erected hidden outposts in numerous other worlds — silent anchor points that release a Guardian when the local Darkness reaches a critical threshold. This world too received such an outpost eons ago — no settlement, no permanent presence, just a brief, targeted intervention. After that it was abandoned, fell into ruin, and was forgotten.

## 7.2 Present Day - Setting

Medieval Fantasy with a Sci-Fi undercurrent. Today's world looks medieval - villages, forests, castles, caves. Hidden beneath it lie the remnants of the ancient outpost. Today's inhabitants know nothing about it anymore; the ruins only appear as Secret Room content and lore fragments, not in NPC dialogue.

Now the Darkness reaches this world too. It spreads, devouring land and color.

## 7.3 Protagonist

Just before the world sinks completely into Darkness, the anchor point of the outpost activates: on a remote beach, amidst a color explosion, a Guardian appears — and pushes the Darkness back a little.

He does not exist as a person with memories of an origin, but as pure, shaped energy. His appearance is not an awakening but a creation: he knows from the start only his duty, not who he really is or exactly where he comes from.

Nobody in today's world knows him or his kind — he is not from here. To NPCs and players alike, he is a mystery.

His sword is not a carried item, but a part of his energy that he only manifests in combat — visible only during the attack animation.

## 7.4 Story Structure

| Chapter | Story Progress |
|---|---|
| Prologue | The Darkness devours the world, a Guardian materializes on the beach in a color explosion and pushes it back, knows only his duty |
| Chap 1 | Fights through the nearby surroundings, recognizes the scale of the Darkness across the kingdom |
| Chap 2 | Darkness spreads further, first fragments of an ancient network surface in Secret Rooms, first allies join |
| Chap 3 | The Darkness reaches its previous peak, more fragments about his own origin, more allies |
| Chap 4 | Final preparations, all allies united, on the eve of the final confrontation |
| Epilogue | Final confrontation with the Darkness itself — it is definitively pushed back from this world. Reveal of who and what the Guardian really is. He dematerializes again — end of game |

## 7.5 Narrative Devices

- Cutscenes between chapters (skippable)
- NPC dialogue in the world
- Found notes and fragments, mainly in Lore-Secrets (see [02_game-structure.md](02_game-structure.md) 2.5) - documented only for now, not yet built:
  - **Terminal/Console** - player presses E, a text overlay opens. Fits the Sci-Fi/high-tech theme of the ancient outpost ruins well. Needs one reusable overlay/reader system; each terminal just supplies its own text content, no per-terminal code.
  - **Letters/Fragments** - a pickup that opens the same overlay reader. Mechanically identical to the Terminal, just a different sprite/trigger.
  - **Environmental storytelling** - the room tells its own story (a broken Guardian statue, an old outpost) - costs only a tileset/decoration piece, no code at all. The cheapest of the three.
